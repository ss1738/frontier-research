# Food & Agriculture — Frontier Unsolved Problems

## TOP 3 GAPS

### 1. Downstream Processing = 85% of Precision Fermentation Cost
- Upstream fermentation: optimizable, commodity-priced
- Extraction/purification: 85% of total manufacturing cost — nobody is solving this as a standalone
- Pichia pastoris secretes at 1-10 g/L for most heterologous proteins; cost-competitive process needs 50+ g/L
- Aqueous two-phase systems + membrane filtration can't achieve neutral-flavor protein isolates in single pass
- **Specific gap**: high-titer (30-50 g/L) secretory pathway engineering + single-step downstream process for food-grade protein at scale. No published complete techno-economic analysis exists.
- **Company unlock**: precision fermentation process platform — licenses high-titer strains + single-step extraction IP. B2B royalties. Nobody has built this as standalone (everyone vertically integrated). TAM: $15-40B by 2035

### 2. Cultivated Meat Vascularization — The 2mm Diffusion Limit
- Oxygen/nutrient diffusion without blood vessels: limited to 200 micrometers
- Above 2mm: interior becomes necrotic. A steak is 20mm thick.
- Scaffold-based: solves structure, not nutrient delivery at scale
- Bioprinted vascular channels: simultaneously need bioink structural integrity + 10-300µm non-collapsing channels + endothelial cells without immune activation — none met simultaneously
- The $63/kg cultivated meat cost target ASSUMES vascularization is solved. It isn't.
- **Wild hypothesis**: Edible fungal mycelium (Pleurotus ostreatus) co-cultured with animal muscle cells as living vascular substitute — hyphal networks (2-10µm diameter) deliver growth media through 3D constructs + contribute fungal protein to final product. Nobody has measured oxygen transport kinetics through hyphal networks in myosatellite cell co-culture.

### 3. Nitrogen Fixation in Non-Legume Crops
- Legumes fix nitrogen via Rhizobium symbiosis. Research since 1970s to transfer to wheat/rice/maize.
- 2024: nitrogenase biosynthesis pathway expressed in rice chloroplasts — but immediately inactivated by O2
- Nitrogenase irreversibly inactivated by O2 at >5nM. Chloroplasts produce oxygen during photosynthesis.
- Oxygen-tolerant nitrogenase analogs (Streptomyces): 100x lower N2-fixing activity
- **Company unlock**: 10-15 year program. First commercial product = Bayer/BASF/Corteva acquisition target ($2-5B). Solves $200B/year synthetic fertilizer market.
