# FR-009: Model Collapse Causal Mechanism — Factorial Design

## Claim
Variance collapse (tail erasure — rare tokens/patterns disappearing from the generated distribution) is the PRIMARY mechanism of model collapse from synthetic data, NOT distributional drift (the generated distribution diverging from the true distribution as a whole).

**If variance collapse is primary**: inverse-frequency reweighting of synthetic data by 1/P_generator(x) before retraining should delay collapse by ≥15% slower perplexity degradation vs unweighted synthetic, in at least 2 of 3 generator temperature conditions. This works because reweighting specifically upweights low-probability (rare) tokens — directly counteracting variance collapse — without changing which data the model sees.

**If drift is primary**: reweighting will have negligible effect because the problem is the generator distribution diverging from real, not the compression of rare events.

## Why this is new
No existing study has run a controlled causal intervention that independently manipulates:
- (a) distributional shift of the synthetic generator (temperature)
- (b) tail frequency of synthetic samples (inverse-frequency reweighting)
- (c) accumulation of real data

with all other variables held constant, across the same model. The field has correlational evidence but no factorial design isolating the mechanism. (Shumailov et al. 2024 Nature, Dohmatob et al. arXiv:2402.07712, arXiv:2410.04840 — none ran this.)

## Design: 2×2×3 factorial
- **Factor A — accumulation**: no_acc (synthetic replaces real) | acc (keep real + add synthetic)
- **Factor B — reweighting**: none | inv_freq (weight by -log P_generator(x))
- **Factor C — temperature**: 0.3 (low diversity, high confidence) | 0.8 (moderate) | 1.5 (high diversity, noisy)

12 conditions × 5 recursive retraining rounds = 60 evaluations.

## Model
- GPT-nano: 4.9M params, 6 layers, D_model=256, 8 heads
- Character-level byte encoding (no tokenizer dependency)
- Trained on TinyStories (800K real tokens)

## Data
- Real: 800,000 tokens from TinyStories (roneneldan/TinyStories)
- Synthetic: model generates 800,000 tokens per round at the specified temperature
- Validation: 40,000 tokens (held-out from real data, never used for training)

## Falsifier
**PRIMARY**: Inverse-frequency reweighting delays collapse by ≥15% (relative reduction in perplexity increase from round 0 to round 5) compared to unweighted synthetic, in at least 2 of 3 temperature conditions.

If this holds: variance collapse IS the primary mechanism → reweighting is the fix.
If this fails: distributional drift dominates → reweighting doesn't help → need different intervention (e.g., diversity penalties, rejection sampling against real distribution).

## Secondary hypotheses
- Accumulation (keeping real data) should reduce collapse in all conditions (sanity check)
- High temperature (1.5) should collapse faster than low temperature (0.3) in unweighted conditions (more diverse generation → less variance collapse? or faster drift?)
- Reweighting should help MORE at low temperature (0.3) because low-temp generation is the most tail-compressed
