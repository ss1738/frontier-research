# Climate & Environment — Frontier Unsolved Problems

## TOP 3 GAPS

### 1. Atmospheric Methane Removal — The Zero-Research High-Leverage Gap
- Methane GWP: 86x CO2 over 20 years. Atmospheric CH4: 1922 ppb (160% above pre-industrial)
- Natural removal: OH radical oxidation (9-year lifetime). Increasing tropospheric OH = faster methane removal.
- Total global research funding on methane removal: <$5M/year vs $20B+ committed to CO2 DAC
- Three approaches — photocatalytic reactors, chlorine-enhanced OH, iron-salt aerosol — each with unresolved problems, zero coordinated research
- Humidity-tolerant photocatalyst (2025 EST): water on surface inhibits methane oxidation even at atmospheric humidity
- **Company unlock**: methane removal credits worth ~30x CO2 credits by GWP. Revenue from carbon credits + government contracts. First mover = standard setter.

### 2. Enhanced Weathering MRV — The Verification Gap
- Spreading basalt/olivine on agricultural fields: 2-4 Gt CO2/year theoretical potential
- Cation-based MRV misses CO2 uptake when cations adsorbed by soil CEC; can overcount when carbonate (not silicate) dissolves
- Inorganic carbon-based approaches: more accurate but require 1-2m depth soil water sampling, sensitive to CO2 degassing artifacts
- 2025 ACS field results: "sustained enhanced weathering signal with limited CO2 removal" — far below model predictions, reasons unknown
- Without reliable MRV: buyers cannot verify what they purchase → market cannot scale
- **Company unlock**: ERW MRV-as-a-service — $5-15/tonne verification fees. Moat: accreditation across Verra/Puro/Gold Standard. The Intertek of carbon removal.

### 3. DAC Sorbent Regeneration — 15-25x Above Thermodynamic Minimum
- Current solid-sorbent DAC: 6-10 MJ/kg CO2 regeneration energy. Thermodynamic minimum: ~0.4 MJ/kg. Gap = 15-25x
- Driven by: water co-adsorption (amine sites adsorb 3-5 water molecules per CO2), amine degradation, heat transfer inefficiency
- Low-temperature sorbents (MOFs, ionic liquids) regenerating at <80°C exist in literature but none simultaneously achieve: >90% CO2 purity + >10,000 cycles + hydrophobic water rejection + CO2 selectivity
- Humidity-swing or pH-swing (not thermal) could cut energy 60-70% and reach $100/tonne
- **Company unlock**: sorbent materials company developing humidity-swing/pH-swing DAC process. Enabling technology for the entire $490/tonne → $100/tonne transition.

## WILD HYPOTHESIS
**Methanotroph augmentation in tropical peatlands**: Tropical peatlands emit ~150 Mt CH4/year. Native methanotroph bacteria (Methylococcus, Methylosinus) oxidize it at the surface. Copper is the active site metal in particulate methane monooxygenase and is methanotroph-limiting in many peatlands. Nobody has run a controlled copper-micronutrient field trial on tropical peatland to measure whether it shifts net methane emission to net-neutral. Cost: tens of dollars per hectare vs thousands per tonne CO2 for DAC. Zero published field trials.
