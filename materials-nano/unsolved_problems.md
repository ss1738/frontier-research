# Materials Science & Nanotechnology — Frontier Unsolved Problems

## TOP 3 GAPS

### 1. Intrinsically Disordered Protein (IDP) Drug Targeting — The Selectivity Catastrophe
- ~30–40% of the human proteome is intrinsically disordered; 80% of cancer-related proteins carry long disordered regions (c-Myc, p53 TAD, α-synuclein, K-Ras4B). None have a stable binding pocket
- IDPs exist as heterogeneous conformational ensembles of rapidly interconverting states on µs–ms timescales. AlphaFold 3 introduced disorder-aware training but DisProt benchmark evaluations expose persistent failures — conformational ensemble not predicted, only a single pseudo-structure
- Key biophysical finding 2024 (bioRxiv 2024.07.03): small-molecule ligands do NOT induce folding. They engage via dynamic networks of transient, weak interactions (Kd typically 10–500 µM range) without creating a discrete bound state. Kills standard structure-based drug design
- Fuzzy complexes: IDPs form "fuzzy" complexes where disordered regions remain disordered even in the bound state (PNAS 2021, Frontiers Mol Biosci 2018). Selectivity is encoded in dynamic, non-random organization of distant interacting regions — physical basis of this selectivity not understood at atomic resolution
- Ensemble docking (bioRxiv 2025.01.23): generate many conformers, dock across them. Can predict relative affinities measured by NMR — but the conformational ensemble must first be accurately sampled, and no method reliably samples IDPs at physiological conditions on drug-relevant timescales (ms to seconds)
- **Specific gap**: No validated, generalizable method to: (a) generate a correct Boltzmann-weighted conformational ensemble for an IDP in the presence of a small molecule, (b) compute selectivity between IDP paralogs sharing <30% sequence identity, or (c) distinguish a genuine allosteric modulator from a promiscuous binder. Every approved IDP-targeting drug was found empirically, not by rational design
- **Company unlock**: First computational platform delivering selective small-molecule hits for a specific IDP with measurable Kd < 1 µM validated by SPR or NMR unlocks a $50B+ oncology target class (c-Myc has been "undruggable" for 40 years). Aileron Therapeutics (stapled peptides) and Monte Rosa Therapeutics (molecular glues) approach it — neither has a general platform

### 2. Wafer-Scale 2D Materials — The Defect Floor Nobody Has Broken
- CVD dominant synthesis route for wafer-scale MoS2, WS2, h-BN. Growth temperatures 700–1000°C — above CMOS BEOL thermal budget of ~400°C. Fundamental integration barrier
- Sulfur vacancy (VS) defect density in as-grown MoS2: 2.71 ± 0.65 × 10¹³ cm⁻². Best oxygen-assisted repair 2024 (PMC11558109): reduced to 4.28 ± 0.27 × 10¹² cm⁻², mobility → 65.2 cm² V⁻¹ s⁻¹
- Mobility target for logic applications: >100 cm² V⁻¹ s⁻¹. Theory requires defect density below ~10¹¹ cm⁻². Best 2024 result is still 40× above this floor
- Samsung demonstrated 200mm wafer-scale MoS2 (May 2024). imec IEDM 2024: monolayer MoS2 stacked nanosheet FETs on 300mm wafers, Ion ~451 µA/µm, on/off ratio >10⁹ — device metrics, material defect problem persists underneath
- Grain boundaries in polycrystalline films cause threshold voltage variability, subthreshold swing degradation, circuit-level yield collapse. 100% single-crystal yield at wafer scale not demonstrated
- Transfer process: dry transfer at 300mm (imec VLSI 2024, >99.5% morphological yield) reduces wrinkles but introduces charge traps at dielectric interface. Trapped charge density 10¹¹–10¹² cm⁻² not stable under operating bias
- **Specific gap**: No synthesis method simultaneously achieves (a) BEOL-compatible temperature ≤450°C, (b) VS defect density <10¹¹ cm⁻², (c) grain boundary density approaching 0 at 300mm wafer scale, and (d) dielectric interface trap density <10¹⁰ cm⁻². All four must be satisfied together for working logic transistor yield >90%. Satisfying any one degrades at least one other
- **Company unlock**: Whoever solves this delivers the gate-all-around transistor channel material for the 1nm node and beyond. Intel, TSMC, Samsung all watching imec's MX2 program. A process IP position here is worth billions — comparable to strained Si IP value in the 2000s

### 3. Solid-State Battery Electrolyte-Electrode Interface — The Space-Charge Layer Is Not What Anyone Thought
- Sulfide solid electrolytes (argyrodite Li6PS5Cl, LGPS): ionic conductivities exceeding 20 mS cm⁻¹ at room temperature — matching or exceeding liquid electrolytes. The bulk conductivity problem is essentially solved
- Interfacial impedance can exceed bulk electrolyte impedance by 100–1000× and drops dramatically after just a few cycles, independent of dendrite growth
- 2023 atomic-scale study (PMC10039002): directly challenged canonical space-charge layer model. Standard model assumed Li-deficient space-charge layer with insufficient charge carriers — but atomic-resolution STEM + EELS shows actual charge distribution and ion transport does not match this picture. Real influence of space-charge layers remains mechanistically unresolved
- Dendrite growth in sulfide electrolytes: different physics than liquid systems. Slow lateral expansion stabilizes while fast vertical eruption cracks ceramic — pressure/temperature phase boundary for this transition not predictively known
- Chemo-mechanical coupling: lithiation/delithiation causes 10–40% volume changes in oxide cathodes (NMC, LCO). In solid-solid contact → contact loss voids → stress concentration → electrolyte fracture. Coupled reaction-diffusion modeled (arXiv:2506.10944) but not validated experimentally with sub-cycle resolution
- No reliable in-situ technique exists to monitor buried solid-solid interface under operating conditions at atomic resolution. Synchrotron X-ray tomography, cryo-TEM, operando Raman all have compromises (beam damage, pressure incompatibility, lateral resolution)
- 200+ coating strategies (LiNbO3, Li3PO4, Al2O3 ALD) with inconsistent results across groups — because failure mechanism is not understood
- **Specific gap**: Identity, spatial distribution, and electrochemical role of interphase phases at NMC|sulfide and Li-metal|sulfide interfaces over the first 10 cycles — not known at atomic resolution. Without this, engineering a stable artificial interlayer is empirical trial-and-error
- **Company unlock**: Solid Power, Solid Energy, Samsung SDI, Toyota all converging on sulfide electrolytes. The company or national lab that generates the definitive atomic-resolution operando dataset of the cathode-electrolyte interphase — and builds a predictive model — controls the next generation EV battery IP. $400B market by 2035

## WILD HYPOTHESIS
**Charged Domain Wall Drift Memory (CDWDM) — The Experiment Nobody Has Run Seriously**: All existing ferroelectric domain wall memory uses stochastic nucleation during switching (write) and domain density (read). Problems: nucleation stochasticity, fatigue after 10⁵–10⁶ cycles, retention loss from depolarization fields. The hypothesis: use the anisotropic drift velocity of a SINGLE charged domain wall under a lateral DC bias — not a switching event — as the write mechanism. In improper ferroelectrics (ErMnO3) or multiferroic BiFeO3 under uniaxial strain, charged domain walls can drift at 1–100 nm/s under moderate fields (1–10 MV/m), deterministically within a pinning-site landscape fixed by crystal structure. Wall position encodes analog state (position along 1D track between two pinning sites = continuous memory value). Conductance at a read electrode varies as the wall moves because local carrier density under a CDW is 10²–10³× that of bulk. The first experiment nobody has run seriously: grow a single-domain BiFeO3 stripe (50 nm width × 500 nm length) between two Pt electrodes, use AFM tip poling to write a single CDW at a defined position, then apply a lateral drift voltage of 2–5 V and read conductance vs time. If drift is deterministic and reproducible: analog position memory with sub-5 nm resolution, no nucleation stochasticity, sub-nanosecond read time (resistive), erase via reversing drift field. Fatigue mechanism disappears because polarization is never switched — only a topological object is slid. This specific combination — single charged head-to-head wall + multiferroic material + sub-100nm geometry with defined pinning sites + analog position encoding — has not appeared as a device concept in 2024–2025 literature. ACS Applied Electronic Materials 2024 review covers digital switching only.
